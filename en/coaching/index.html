Coaching

One-to-one work for leaders

You have a specific issue: a conversation you've been avoiding. A decision that's hanging. A role you haven't fully stepped into yet.

I don't use personality tests or lengthy background analyses. We look at what's actually happening – in the situation, in the conversation, in the dynamic – and work from there.

Typical topics

  • → Preparing a difficult conversation with a team member
  • → Taking on a new leadership role
  • → Clarifying a negotiation situation
  • → Conflict with a manager or peer
  • → Recognising and shifting communication patterns
Format

60–90 minutes per session, online or in Berlin. Typically 3–6 sessions depending on the topic.

How I work

Direct, clear, focused on action

I don't simply validate what you tell me. If I see something you're not seeing, I'll say so. That's not confrontation – it's the difference between coaching and a good conversation with a friend.

Methods

What I draw on

Systemic coaching, FEED model (feedback), EAT model (listening), Difficult Conversations (Stone/Patton/Heen), Harvard negotiation principles.

Mediation

When teams are stuck

Some conflicts don't resolve through direct conversation between the parties – because the conversation itself is part of the problem. That's when you need someone who holds the process without taking sides.

As a mediator, I facilitate clarification processes between individuals, teams or departments. The goal isn't harmony. It's working capacity – and a shared understanding of how to move forward.

Typical situations

  • → Persistent tensions within a team
  • → Conflict between a leader and team members
  • → Cross-departmental friction
  • → Situations where HR is already involved
Format

Preliminary conversations with each party, then joint sessions. Duration depends on complexity. On-site preferred.

Conflict as a third entity

A conflict isn't the sum of two bad intentions. It's an independent phenomenon that pulls both parties into its logic – often without anyone wanting it to.

My work addresses that dynamic (drawing on Eidenschink and Glasl): making the conflict visible as a third presence in the room, before we talk about solutions.
