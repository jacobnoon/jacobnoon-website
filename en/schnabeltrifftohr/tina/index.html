Self-revelation Ear

Tina, the Mirror

Tina (or Till) hears I-messages and actively reflects them back to the speaker. She listens with fine antennae for what perhaps was never meant to be said – but slipped out anyway.

"Sounds like you might wish for more … ?"

Play recommendations

  • Consistently interpret statements as self-revelation.
  • Mirror suspected feelings and inner states.
  • Ask about needs, motives and inner sensations.
  • Steer conversations toward the emotional experience.
  • Ignore the content and appeal level in favour of the other person's inner perspective.
👉 Rule of thumb: The other's inner world before content and appeal.

Body language

  • Soft, open posture.
  • Calm, attentive gesture.
  • Lots of eye contact, searching and inviting.
  • Slightly tilted head, empathetic expression.
  • Small physical distance – closeness is offered.

Signature lines

  • "I get the impression this is on your mind?"
  • "There seems to be something underneath that for you, isn't there?"
  • "How is this really for you?"
  • "I wonder what this does to you."
  • "It sounds like you're torn inside?"
  • "Sounds like you simply feel… …"
