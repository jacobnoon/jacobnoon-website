Conflict Management

Conflict isn't a malfunction. It's information. Those who can read what a conflict is showing them can intervene early – before escalation takes over the conversation.

In this training, leaders and teams learn to recognise conflict at different levels (content, relationship, values), assess escalation dynamics, and lead conversations that create clarity without destroying trust.

Glasl model Difficult Conversations Role plays with real cases 2–3 days
02

Negotiation

Most negotiations don't fail because of the offer. They fail because positions are defended instead of interests being explored.

This training is grounded in Harvard principles and makes them practical: developing your BATNA, uncovering interests behind positions, handling difficult counterparts without capitulating.

Harvard principles BATNA Negotiation simulations 2 days
03

Difficult Conversations & Leadership Communication

Giving feedback someone actually hears. Telling a team member something isn't working. Running a team briefing that lands. These conversations share one challenge: how do you communicate with clarity without damaging trust?

The systemic starting point: organisations consist, at their core, of communication. Leadership communication is therefore not a soft-skill add-on – it is the actual job of leadership. Leaders connect people to the organisation and make decisions they must communicate. Whether that works depends not on personality, but on stance and craft.

The training combines the FEED model with the four-sides model (Schulz von Thun) and a needs-based communication approach rooted in NVC. Concretely: What happened? What do I feel – and what does that say about my assumptions? What do I need, and what does the other person need? Participants bring their own situations. We work through them. The communication game "Schnabel trifft Ohr" adds an experiential layer.

FEED model Four-sides model NVC / needs-based approach Difficult Conversations Systemic leadership (Seliger) Schnabel trifft Ohr 1–2 days
04

Presenting with Impact

Presentations that actually reach people work like good stories. This training brings storytelling techniques from improvisation theatre into the context of pitches, briefings and internal communication.

Storytelling Impro methods Body language 1–2 days
05

Applied Improvisation

Improvisation theatre is more than a creativity method. It's a training lab for the mindsets modern leadership requires: listening before responding, tolerating uncertainty, reacting to what's actually happening rather than what you expected.

The core is the Yes-And principle: accept what's offered, build on it, develop it further. At the same time, Applied Improvisation trains the opposite: when is a clear no the stronger response? Where do my own boundaries lie? The exercises come from professional impro theatre – and make visible in a short time what seminars often spend weeks on theoretically.

Yes-And principle Boundary awareness Presence & listening Dealing with uncertainty ½–2 days

How I work

No two trainings work alike.

Before every in-house format, I run a scoping conversation. What's the specific situation? Who's the target group? What should be different afterwards? That conversation shapes the design – not a catalogue entry.

All trainings follow the same principle: experience before theory. Participants live it first, then we frame it. Max. 3 minutes of lecture input at a stretch, then discussion or exercise.
