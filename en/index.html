My approach

Experience before theory. Always.

Most training starts with the slide. I start with the conflict. If you hear a model before you have wrestled with what it is for, you do not actually hear it. If you have felt how the same situation can tip three different ways, you listen to the model like an answer.

Three pillars carry the work. Improvisational theatre: working with what is actually happening in the room, not with what you prepared. Nonviolent Communication after Rosenberg: separating trigger from cause, finding the needs underneath the behaviour. Lived leadership experience: as founder and CEO of a tech startup, I did not learn about conflict in a seminar. I learned it in the role itself. I recognise what you are describing.

Negotiation

Most negotiations don't fail at the offer. They fail when positions get defended. We train what lies underneath.
